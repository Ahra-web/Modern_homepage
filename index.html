Modern design is a design philosophy that began in the early 20th century and is a style that developed around simplicity, functionality, and minimalism. This is an approach that excludes unnecessary decoration or complexity and emphasizes the essential purpose and usability of the design. Modern design is used in various fields such as architecture, interior design, graphic design, and web design, and follows the principle of “form follows function”. This means that appearance is determined by the actual use and purpose of the design.

Modern design contrasts with the complexity of Victorian or Baroque styles, which feature traditional ornamentation. After the Industrial Revolution, as efficiency and mass production became important values ​​along with technological advancement, the need for simple and practical design emerged.

Modern design is a philosophical approach based on simplicity, functionality, and practicality, which serves as the overall foundation of modern design. We place importance on practical usability and user experience, not just aesthetics, and this has become a sustainable design philosophy that transcends time and trends.
